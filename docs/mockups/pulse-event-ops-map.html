Howler Pulse · Event Ops concept

The whole site.
One pulse.

A live command map of the event: every station (gates, bars, top-ups, food court, medical) streams its device health into Pulse. When something drifts (a scanner dies, a queue builds, a bar runs dry) the incident fires on the map, the Owl triages it in the console, an operator dispatches, and you watch the runner physically cross the site until the station goes green again. Radio calls from staff land in the same feed.

Full-screen on the ops-room TV: map left, triage feed right, radio calls inline. Designed to be readable from across a container office.

Placement 03Sales deck - the ops story

The "we run your event day" slide: incident fires, Owl triages, runner crosses the map, station goes green. Thirty seconds, no words needed.

Implementation notes: one <canvas> over the stage for telemetry dust, health sweeps, incident ripples and staff-unit movement; the map is a lightweight SVG plan + DOM station chips (ring = % of devices online). Incident engine cycles per phase: fire → triage card → operator action → staff unit travels → resolved. Self-contained, no libraries; respects prefers-reduced-motion; stacks map-over-console on phones. Stations and names are illustrative, modelled on the real Event Ops module (stations · devices · issues).

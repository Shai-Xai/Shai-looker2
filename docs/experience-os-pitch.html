The vision

The Experience Operating System for events.

Every number, document, task and conversation about an event — in one place. Tracked, searchable, actionable. Organisers keep talking wherever they already do; the Owl is always in the room, turning it into a single source of truth.

The problem

Half the relationship lives outside the platform.

Pulse already gives organisers live dashboards, an AI analyst that briefs them every morning, and interactive settlements. But everything the account managers and ops team do to make an event happen — contracts, on-sale setup, pricing approvals, staffing, devices, briefing packs, resale, debriefs — runs on memory, spreadsheets, scattered email and WhatsApp groups. Untracked. Dependent on individual heroics. That's the half we bring in.

The shape

Three layers, one spine.

Tasks and messages are the same primitive — a task is just a thread with a due date and a done‑state. Build the spine once; everything rides it.

📋

The Playbook

What needs doing.
  • Every readiness task, captured once as a reusable template
  • Owner (organiser / AM / ops), due date anchored to the event's phases
  • Some tasks verify themselves from live data — “on sale” ticks when the first ticket sells
💬

The Comms Spine

What's being said.
  • Announcements organisers must acknowledge — captured who & when
  • Threads attached to tasks, settlements & documents
  • Ingests email, Slack & forwarded WhatsApp — we don't fight the channel war
🦉

The Owl

The memory & the voice.
  • Folds ops into the morning briefing
  • Extracts commitments & decisions from messages → suggested tasks
  • Recalls “what did we agree about staffing?” — with the source cited

The approach

Ingestion‑first. CC the Owl.

We don't make anyone change where they talk — we capture it. Every event gets an Owl address; the habit becomes “CC the Owl on everything.”

How we deliver

In slices — each useful on its own.

Messaging spine + inbox + notification bell — unify the notes & Investigate we already have.
Announcements with must‑acknowledge — the cheapest, highest‑leverage “wow”.
Owl email ingestion + event timeline — starts collecting knowledge immediately.
Playbook tasks — built from the team workshop output.
Outbound nudges via the mailer — reach organisers who aren't logged in; replies land back in Pulse.
Owl intelligence — ops in briefings → suggested tasks from messages → “ask the record”.

Where the team comes in

The build is the easy part.

The playbook lives in the AMs' and ops team's heads. One working session turns that into the system's foundation — we've pre‑seeded ~20 tasks to react to.

1 · Attend the workshop

Bring your real checklists, templates and horror stories.

2 · Correct the seed tasks

They're educated guesses — reacting is faster than recalling.

3 · Adopt “CC the Owl”

The system is only as smart as what it sees.

4 · Tell us where it'll break

Postponements, multi‑day festivals, difficult clients — design for them now.

The payoff

Pulse tells organisers what's happening.
Now it tells them what to do about it — and remembers everything.

Nothing falls through the cracks. Nothing gets lost. Organisers feel served, not chased. Handovers stop hurting.

Back-office efficiency · Account management

Hours of manual work, done in minutes.

Today, servicing a client means hopping between Looker, Howler, multiple third-party mailing platforms (each bolted on for a different function) and manual exports into Meta & TikTok. Every handoff costs an account manager time — and several tasks are simply impossible without a workaround. Pulse OS collapses it all into one governed platform, where the audiences and campaigns run on the same data that powers the dashboards.

Why this matters beyond the clock

The time savings are the headline — but three structural shifts matter just as much.
01

Impossible → standard

Per-client branding, custom-list email, paid-social audience sync and multi-campaign reporting weren't slow before — they didn't exist. Pulse makes them one-click and repeatable.

02

Reactive → proactive

The daily email no longer just reports — it recommends actions. AMs move from data janitors to strategic advisors.

03

Fragmented → unified

Removing the Looker → Howler → export → Meta/TikTok handoffs removes the error surface entirely. Live segments, live reporting, no version drift.

The capacity argument

What ~11 hours → ~30 minutes actually means for the business.
~20×

more AM capacity on these tasks

If a full servicing cycle drops from ~11 hours to ~30 minutes, a single account manager's effective capacity on this work multiplies. In practice that means:

  • Onboard more clients without hiring.
  • Reallocate AM time from manual assembly to client strategy and upsell.
  • Offer new paid services — branded dashboards, retargeting audiences, proactive insights — that were previously undeliverable.

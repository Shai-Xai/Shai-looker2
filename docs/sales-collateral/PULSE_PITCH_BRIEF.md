# CLAUDE.md — Howler Pulse · Pitch & Deck Workspace

## What this repo is
Sales/pitch material for **Pulse**, the intelligence layer of **Howler** (howler.co.za),
an Experience Operating System for live events (ticketing + cashless + SuperApp + VIP,
all feeding one governed dataset).

## The golden rule (non-negotiable)
**Every deck or artifact we share with a potential client is PUBLIC.** Assume any client,
prospect, or competitor can read it. Give the **meat** (what the product does for the
organiser, the outcomes, the value) — never the **bones** (how it's built).

### NEVER put in any client-facing / public artifact:
- **Vendor / stack names:** Looker, Anthropic, Resend, Clickatell, Inventive, or any
  named third-party tool. Say "our AI analyst", "email & SMS", "BI layer" — not the vendor.
- **Architecture specifics:** OAuth 2.1 / PKCE, MCP server, `/api/v1`, one-iframe embed,
  Looker explores, row-level opt-in, etc. Describe the *capability*, not the mechanism.
- **Feature-maturity labels:** live / beta / needs-setup / soon. Present the product as one
  coherent offering, not a graded build register.
- **Roadmap:** "coming soon" / "on the horizon" items. Roadmap is shared verbally, under
  NDA, in a live conversation — never on a public page or in a sendable file.
- **Raw client data** belonging to a client other than the deck's recipient.

### The test for any line
"Does this shorten a competitor's *build*, or only their *understanding*?"
Understanding = safe to include. Build-shortening = cut it or reword to outcome-only.

## The 4-tier discipline (from strategy work)
- **Tell** (anyone): vision, the loop, outcomes/benefits.
- **Show** (demo live, don't hand over): the working product; conceptual flow diagrams.
- **Send** (durable artifact they keep): only things safe to be forwarded to a competitor.
- **Only-us** (never leaves): the how, the roadmap, the maturity, the data.

## What is SAFE and should stay LOUD
- The vision and the "insight → action → results → improvement" loop.
- Outcomes ("audiences that re-resolve at send time", "ROAS next to ticket sales",
  "abandoned-cart recovery that closes the goal").
- **The integrated stack** (ticketing + cashless + SuperApp + VIP → one dataset). This is
  the real moat and is copy-PROOF — knowing it doesn't let anyone execute it. Lead with it.
- Security *as a trust signal* ("scoped to you, server-side, fails closed, revocable keys")
  — the value is in the implementation, not the one-line claim.
- Conceptual flow diagrams (side / top-down / radial / ops map).

## Audiences (decks differ ONLY where incentives differ)
1. **Prospective clients / their boards** — may not know Howler at all. Deck must establish
   who Howler is, the problem, why the integrated stack wins, proof, and "why us not the
   competitor". Sell hard; stay public-safe.
2. **Fever (strategic investor AND competitor)** — lead with the stack (thesis + copy-proof)
   and traction; demo live; discuss roadmap only verbally under NDA. Never hand over the
   maturity-graded feature register.
